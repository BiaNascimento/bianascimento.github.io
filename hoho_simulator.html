About Hoho simulator

Role: Solo dev.

Responsabilities: create the whole game on a pre-determined window of time.

Check the game

Hoho Simulator is the first game that I developed alone. It was developed for the #1JAM, a monthly local game jam.

The proposal of the month was to play Secret Santa with the participants, so the challenge was to make a game based on the request of a random participant and delivery this game for him or she by the end of the deadline.

The request that I received was to make a simulation game, and due to the Christmas season I decided to make a Santa Claus simulator, where you need to manage the elfs, decide which kid will receive a gift, take decisions based on the news and relax by playing a silly game.

There were two major challenges on this: manage my time and work on development areas that I'm not used to, as programming and art.

Manage my time was less difficult than I anticipated. For being a long game jam, with roughly one month of duration, I could work on a small scope and even add more features as the development was happening.

I wasn't this successful with art, but I tried to use simple icons and draws.

Design Process

Brainstorming

As it was a solo challenge, I did a brainstorming session using Miro to write down ideas and have a nice view of the most viable.

Early Prototypes

As the game jam process allows, I did a few concept validations with friends and tested with really primitive prototypes.

Playtest

The game passed through some early stage tests with friends and me, and I could trace a more accurate path for the development.

Final version

After the iteration, the game received its final version, with more balancing and features that would improve the player experience
