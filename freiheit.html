About Freiheit

Role: game designer and producer.

Responsabilities: design rules and mechanics for the game, create the user guide, organize and manage the team.

Freiheit, which means "freedom" in German, is a board game about the second World War.

It was my first college project, where, in group, we needed to create a meaningful board game, with the mission to teach people about something.

We chose to teach about Nazism and its horrors, to aware specially young students to never make the same mistake again.

On the game, which was made for 4 or 6 players, some players are prisoners and others are guards. The guards can't let the prisoners leave, and the prisoners need to escape.

Freiheit game design is asymmetric, which means that the guards have more advantage during the gameplay, and that was a purposeful design choice to make the players feels like this is an unfair game.

The difficulties of designing Freiheit included to be careful to not romanticize the atrocities of this period and be respectful with the victims, as well as being able to take the player through a reflection state while playing it, instead of designing the game for the polemic.

Design Process

Brainstorming

The team performed brainstorm sessions, where we defined the theme of the game, as well as the main mechanics.

Early Prototypes

We developed an early paper prototype, with primitive components, to test our early ideas and grids for the game.

Playtest

The game was tested and we could evaluate which practices and mechanics were working.

New version

We developed a new version of Freiheit, taking into consideration the feedback received during the playtests, and now we had a different and improved version.

Last playtest

The last playtest was made, to polish minor mistakes.

Instruction Manual Finalization

The Instruction Manual was finalized, based on the changes that occurred during the playtests.
